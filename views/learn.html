Supporting Scholarly Research

The Ancient Egyptian Museum is committed to providing comprehensive resources and support for academics, researchers, and students interested in Egyptology and ancient civilizations. Our extensive collection and dedicated staff are available to assist with your scholarly endeavors.

Research Access Programs

The museum offers specialized access programs designed for academic researchers requiring in-depth study of our collections. These programs provide extended hours, close examination opportunities, and consultation with our curatorial team.

Our Research Access Programs are available to qualified academics from accredited institutions and independent scholars with established research portfolios. Applications are reviewed quarterly by our Academic Committee.

Benefits include:

  • Access to non-exhibited artifacts in our repository
  • Permission to photograph artifacts for scholarly publications
  • Consultation with museum specialists and conservators
  • Access to our digital archives and historical documentation
  • Dedicated workspace in our research center

Digital Resources

Our digital repository contains extensive documentation of artifacts, historical records, and scholarly publications related to our collections. These resources are invaluable for researchers unable to visit in person or those requiring preliminary research before an on-site visit.

The digital collection includes:

  • High-resolution images of over 15,000 artifacts
  • 3D scans of selected monumental pieces
  • Translated papyri and inscription databases
  • Archaeological field reports from 1880 to present
  • Conservation and restoration documentation

Access to our comprehensive digital resources requires academic credentials verification. Please submit your request through our academic portal or contact our research department directly.

"The study of ancient Egyptian civilization offers us profound insights into human development, technological innovation, and cultural evolution. Our institution is dedicated to facilitating this important scholarly work."

— Dr. Sarah Mahmoud, Head of Research, The Ancient Egyptian Museum

Fellowship and Grant Opportunities

The museum administers several fellowship and grant programs to support original research related to our collections. These competitive programs provide financial support, access to resources, and mentorship from our curatorial staff.

Doctoral Research Fellowships

Annual fellowships for PhD candidates conducting dissertation research in Egyptology, archaeology, art history, or related fields. Fellows receive:

  • Stipend of $25,000 for one academic year
  • Research expense allowance of $3,000
  • Priority access to collections and archives
Senior Scholar Grants

Designed for established academics pursuing specific research projects related to our collections. Grants provide:

  • Research funding up to $15,000
  • Up to six months of access to museum resources
  • Publication support through our academic press

Applications for all fellowship and grant programs must include a detailed research proposal, curriculum vitae, and letters of recommendation. Submission deadlines occur biannually on March 15 and September 15.

Specialized Library Collection

Our research library houses one of the most comprehensive collections of Egyptological literature in the world. The collection includes rare manuscripts, expedition reports, out-of-print publications, and contemporary scholarly journals.

The library catalog contains:

  • Over 45,000 volumes on Egyptian archaeology
  • Complete sets of major Egyptological journals
  • Rare books dating to the 18th century
  • Expedition notebooks and unpublished manuscripts
  • Microfiche archive of historical documentation
  • Specialized linguistics and hieroglyphic resources

Library access is available to researchers by appointment. Reference librarians specialized in Egyptology are available for consultation Tuesday through Saturday from 10 AM to 4 PM.

Conferences & Symposia

The museum hosts regular academic conferences, workshops, and symposia on topics related to ancient Egyptian civilization. These events bring together scholars from around the world to share research and foster collaboration.

Upcoming academic events:

  • September 2025: Workshop on "Conservation Methods for Organic Materials"
  • November 2025: International Symposium: "Technology and Innovation in Ancient Egypt"

Call for papers and registration information are available through our academic affairs office.
